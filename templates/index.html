About Us

VIBERENT is a project funded by the National Science Foundation Innovation Corps (NSF I-Corp) and their partnership with University of California San Diego.

We are conducting research on the fashion industry's sustainable practices and initiatives.

Our research looks to understand the current available solutions to the industry's "take, make, waste" linear model in hopes to find the missing piece and help to develop a circular model with zero waste. We are interested in interviewing brands that are sustainably driven, non-profit organizations advocating for sustainable and ethical practices, and customers that are sustainably conscious.

We are currently writing a project pitch to apply for further NSF funding.

Meet the Researchers

Diana Valdes

UCSD Alumni, B.A. Sociology ‘19

Diana brings in a sociological perspective that allows her to see the bigger picture. Her research focuses on the intersectionality of environmental and socioeconomic impacts. Diana’s passion grew from experiencing first hand the impacts of the industry’s unethical practices in developing countries.

Tiffany Wang

UCSD Alumni, B.S. Chemical Engineering ‘20

Tiffany’s research focuses on analyzing responsible, resource-efficient approaches to production and consumption that do not rely on sacrificing a country’s environmental sustainability for a company’s economic sustainability. She is excited to use her technical background to examine the flow of materials in fashion’s complex supply chain and identify opportunities to close the loop.
